Matrix

Year: 2004
Duration: 1h 41m
Rating: R
Ranking: #5 in Movies Today

The Matrix is a 1999 science fiction action film about a computer hacker who discovers that his reality is a deception by an evil cyber-intelligence

Cast: Keanu Reeves as Neo: A hacker searching for the truth about reality and his role in freeing humanity from the Matrix. Reeves won the role over several major stars, including Johnny Depp and Leonardo DiCaprio. Laurence Fishburne as Morpheus: The captain of the Nebuchadnezzar and Neo's mentor, guiding him to fulfill his destiny. Fishburne based his performance on the Sandman character of the same name. Carrie-Anne Moss as Trinity: A skilled fighter and Neo’s love interest, freed from the Matrix by Morpheus. Moss underwent rigorous training for the role, which launched her career. Hugo Weaving as Agent Smith: A program designed to maintain control of the Matrix, with a unique ambition to break free. Weaving crafted a distinctive voice inspired by the Wachowskis’ own tones. Joe Pantoliano as Cypher: A disillusioned crewmember of the Nebuchadnezzar who betrays the rebels to return to the Matrix. He previously worked with the Wachowskis on Bound. Marcus Chong as Tank: The Nebuchadnezzar's operator and a natural-born human who was raised outside the Matrix. Matt Doran as Mouse: A programmer on the Nebuchadnezzar, known for his curiosity and inventive nature. Rowan Witt as Spoon Boy: A young prophet in the Matrix who teaches Neo about bending reality. Gloria Foster as The Oracle: A wise prophet living within the Matrix, offering guidance and foresight to the rebels. Anthony Ray Parker as Dozer: The Nebuchadnezzar's pilot and Tank’s brother, both born naturally outside the Matrix. Julian Arahanga as Apoc: A freed human and crewmember of the Nebuchadnezzar. Belinda McClory as Switch: A confident and stylish member of Morpheus’s crew. Paul Goddard as Agent Brown & Robert Taylor as Agent Jones: Sentient programs assisting Agent Smith in maintaining the Matrix and eliminating threats. Ada Nicodemou as DuJour: A minor character who briefly interacts with Neo and hints at his journey.

Director: The Wachowskis- Lana Wachowski and lili Wachowski
